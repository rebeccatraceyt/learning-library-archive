Diffchecker

A little tool that makes a big difference

What is a diffchecker?

A diffchecker displays two files side-by-side, with any differences between the two highlighted. This allows you to compare your code to either similar code, solution code, or past file revisions to check for errors, typos, or omissions.

For example, you could use a diffchecker to find typos in a Django settings.py file by comparing your file with solution source code from GitHub.

Our recommended diffchecker

We recommend that students use diffchecker.com as they work through their walkthrough projects. If you have an error that you can’t track down, comparing your code with the solution code provided in the lesson is an excellent first step.

Keep the diffchecker open beside your IDE, and you’ll soon cut your troubleshooting time in half!

How do you diffcheck?

1 Go to diffchecker.com
2 Paste the solution code in the left window named: (Original Text)
3 Paste your code into the right window named: (Changed Text)
4 Click the Find Difference button
5 View your results to compare your code

Share your diffs!

You can share your diffchecker results with others (or save the link for yourself!). To do that:

1 Under the Changed Text window, click the Share button
2 Give your diff a title (make it memorable) and click Save
3 Select who can view, click Copy, and a link to your diff will be copied to your clipboard, ready to be pasted wherever you wish to share it
