5 Planes of UX

Jesse James Garrett introduced us to the five planes of UX design in his book called The Elements of User Experience. It is an abstract framework that breaks down the design experience into manageable components. This is a great guide to follow when planning your project. The planes are strategy, scope, structure, skeleton, and surface.

Strategy

In this plane, you can brainstorm the featured components that are necessary to meet your user stories and product objectives. This is the most abstract and least constrained part of the project, where you can keep in mind the most important and viable features that will allow for a more concise, user-friendly design.

Scope

The scope plane is necessary to help identify the project's needs, aligning the strategised features. This is broken into content and functionality requirements. This plane is used to develop a scalable list of requirements for the project.

Structure

In the structure plane you can plan the Information Architecture, to improve findability and usability for users. You can do this by creating a flow navigation chart to illustrate the ideal flow of the site.

Skeleton

During the skeleton plane you can create grey scale wireframes for different device sizes by adding in the components and content that were prioritised in previous planning stages.

Surface

Using the previous planes as a foundation, you can create mockup versions of the above wireframes to reflect the desired end product. This is where the typography, styling and components are strategically designed to optimise the user experience.
